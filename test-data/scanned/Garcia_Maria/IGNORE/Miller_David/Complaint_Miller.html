COMPLAINT

Plaintiff, DAVID MILLER (“Plaintiff”), by and through his undersigned counsel, brings this action against Defendants AMERICAN EXPRESS COMPANY (“American Express”), and TRANS UNION, LLC (“Trans Union”), and alleges the following:

PRELIMINARY STATEMENT

1. This is an action for damages arising from Defendants’ reporting of inaccurate information on Plaintiff’s credit report and their failure to conduct a reasonable investigation into his disputes.

2. Defendants are reporting a late payment on an American Express account that was, in fact, paid on time.

3. As a result of this single inaccuracy, Plaintiff’s credit score dropped significantly, causing him to be denied a favorable interest rate on a personal loan.

JURISDICTION AND VENUE

4. Jurisdiction is conferred by 15 U.S.C. § 1681p and 28 U.S.C. § 1331.

5. Venue is proper in this District as Plaintiff resides here and the harm occurred here.

PARTIES

6. Plaintiff, David Miller, is a natural person residing in the borough of Brooklyn, State of New York.

7. Defendant American Express Company is a “furnisher of information” under the FCRA.

8. Defendant Trans Union, LLC is a “consumer reporting agency” as defined by the FCRA.

FACTUAL BACKGROUND

9. On March 15, 2025, Plaintiff made a timely payment to his American Express account. Due to a processing error on the part of American Express, the payment was not credited to his account until March 18, 2025, making it appear as if he was three days late.

10. American Express subsequently furnished this inaccurate "30 days late" information to Trans Union, who then reported it on Plaintiff's credit report.

11. On April 10, 2025, Plaintiff disputed the late payment with both American Express and Trans Union, providing a copy of his bank statement showing the payment was made on time.

12. Both Defendants failed to conduct a reasonable investigation. American Express simply verified the date the payment was posted, not the date it was made. Trans Union merely "parroted" this incorrect verification.

13. The inaccurate late payment remains on his Trans Union credit report, damaging his creditworthiness.

CAUSE OF ACTION - VIOLATIONS OF THE FCRA

14. Plaintiff realleges all prior paragraphs.

15. Trans Union willfully or negligently violated 15 U.S.C. § 1681e(b) and § 1681i.

16. American Express willfully or negligently violated 15 U.S.C. § 1681s-2(b).

17. As a result of these violations, Plaintiff has suffered actual damages and is entitled to actual, statutory, and punitive damages, plus attorney's fees and costs.

DEMAND FOR JURY TRIAL

Plaintiff demands a trial by jury.
