CASE BACKGROUND:
Sarah Johnson contacted our office after being denied for a mortgage loan due to inaccurate information on her credit report. The credit report from TransUnion showed a delinquent account with First National Bank that was never hers.

TIMELINE OF EVENTS:
- March 15, 2025: Client applies for mortgage loan
- March 20, 2025: Loan denied due to delinquent account on credit report
- March 25, 2025: Client disputes account with TransUnion
- April 10, 2025: TransUnion responds claiming account verified
- April 15, 2025: Client contacts First National Bank directly
- April 20, 2025: First National Bank confirms no account exists for client
- May 1, 2025: Client re-disputes with TransUnion providing bank letter
- May 15, 2025: TransUnion again claims account verified
- June 1, 2025: Client retains our office

DEFENDANTS:
1. First National Bank of New York
   - 456 Financial Plaza, New York, NY 10005
   - Failed to properly investigate dispute
   
2. TransUnion LLC
   - 555 W. Adams Street, Chicago, IL 60661
   - Failed to conduct reasonable investigation
   - Continued reporting inaccurate information

FCRA VIOLATIONS:
- Section 1681e(b) - Failure to follow reasonable procedures
- Section 1681i(a) - Failure to conduct reasonable investigation
- Section 1681n - Willful non-compliance

DAMAGES:
- Denial of mortgage loan
- Damage to credit score (dropped from 750 to 620)
- Emotional distress and frustration
- Time and effort spent disputing
- Lost opportunity to purchase home at favorable interest rate

RELIEF SOUGHT:
- Actual damages
- Statutory damages up to $1,000 per defendant
- Attorney fees and costs
- Injunctive relief requiring correction of credit report
